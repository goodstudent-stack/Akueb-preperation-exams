Quadratic Equations - Explanation
Class 10

A quadratic equation is an equation of the form ax² + bx + c = 0, where a, b, and c are constants and a ≠ 0.

Methods to Solve Quadratic Equations:
  1. Factorization: Express the quadratic as a product of two linear factors.
  2. Quadratic Formula: x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a
  3. Completing the Square: Rewrite the equation in the form (x + p)² = q
Example from AKUEB 2022 Paper:

Solve the equation: 2x² - 5x - 3 = 0

Solution:

Using factorization: (2x + 1)(x - 3) = 0

Therefore, x = -1/2 or x = 3
